How it works

Slot-based only

Every item occupies exactly one slot regardless of size. No weight, no encumbrance — only slot count matters.
Stack overflow

Cap is 50 per slot

Mats, food and ammo stack to 50. Carrying 51+ of anything bleeds into a second slot. Craft often to consolidate.
◈
The Group feature consolidates same-type items visually — it doesn't increase your slot count, but makes the inventory screen much cleaner to navigate.
⚠
Shared inventory across Kliff, Damiane and Oongka. You can park a secondary character near a vendor to sell without fast-travelling yourself.

Trading system overview
⚠
Traders only buy in bulk — you need at least 25 of a specific item per sale at Trading Posts. Single items go to Black Markets at much lower rates.
Step 01
Collect Trade Goods
Blue items found in chests, tables, cabinets while exploring. High-value: Calligraphic Paintings, Tobacco, Ceramics, Red Ginseng.
Step 02
Glenbright Manor
Complete the investigation of Glenbright Manor story quest first — this unlocks the ability to package goods at camp.
Step 03
Package via Carl
Carl (Provisions Keeper) at Greymane Camp → Manage Provisions → Package. Costs 100 Copper per item.
Step 04
Load Wagon via Brice
Talk to Brice to get your wagon. Load packaged goods from inventory or camp storage. Build Brice's workshop to upgrade wagon capacity.
Step 05
Drive to Trading Post
Check map for "High" price percentage before committing to a route. Goldleaf Guildhouse unlocks after Kailok the Hornsplitter boss.
✓
Never return with an empty wagon. Buy local goods from the destination post and route to another — that's where trading income compounds. Check the map's "High" price filter before you drive.

Key mechanics the game doesn't explain well
01
Waterfall traversal

Waterfalls will knock you back if you walk through them. Use Stab (RB+Y / R1+△ / LSHIFT+RMB) right in front of the waterfall — Kliff parts the water with his blade and passes through. There's almost always treasure behind them.

02
Stacked rock towers = hidden treasure

If you see a small tower of stacked rocks in front of a waterfall or cliff face, that marks a passage or hidden chest. Either unique equipment or rare ore — always worth checking.

03
Blinding Flash has hidden uses

Beyond combat, Blinding Flash can set environmental objects (vines, barriers) on fire. Just aim the light at the obstacle. Also cooks meat if you drop it on the ground — no campfire needed.

04
Focus (slow-time) — don't forget it exists

Kliff's slow-time ability is introduced during a busy cutscene and never mentioned again. Bind it to an accessible button and use it liberally — it creates windows to land heavy combos or dodge otherwise unavoidable hits.

05
Sky Islands for fast travel shortcuts

On the map press R3 (PC: Mouse Wheel) to view the sky map. You can skydive from the nearest sky island fast travel point to any nearby ground location quickly — faster than riding for frequently visited spots without ground Nexuses.

06
Height climbing without Stamina drain

Use double jump + three Aerial Force Palms to reach considerable height without spending any Stamina. If stamina runs out mid-climb, immediately eat any stamina-restoring food — it refills instantly and you can continue.

07
Boss strategy that always works

Bosses are designed so you WILL take damage. Heal while attacking with light attacks. For blockable (human) bosses: parry with L1/LB, then combo. For shield users: R2/RT heavy attacks break guard. For monsters: dodge combos, punish windows. Bring at least 50 cooked food and hold Palmar Pills for the final phase.

08
Hold attack, don't tap

Hold R1/RB for continuous attacks instead of tapping each time. Much more efficient combat rhythm — tapping is the default instinct but holding keeps your combo momentum going.

09
Best early skills to unlock

Nature's Echo, Quick Swap, and Blinding Flash are the highest-priority early unlocks. Best early weapons: Tauria's Curved Sword (Crowcaller boss) and Sword of the Lord (Kailok boss). Play on controller if on PC — KB+M is notably harder.

10
Check Notifications for missed knowledge

Open Notifications from the main menu — it lists everything that's appeared and can redirect you to the specific menu section if you missed a knowledge entry or objective. Use this to stay organised as the quest list balloons.

Only healing source — non-negotiable

Cook at any pot or campfire. Eat mid-combat with no cooldown and no animation lock. Keep 100+ Grilled Meat on you for any boss. Cooked meals give significantly more HP than raw food. Meat Skewers restore both Health and Spirit for one extra ingredient.

How to tame horses

Standard horse taming

Wild horses roam open plains and river areas, particularly east and south of Hernand. Approach slowly — moving too fast scares them away. Once close, mount and the taming minigame begins.

The trick: Hold the camera directly behind the horse and hold S (backward) continuously. The game says to align WASD with the horse's tail — holding the camera behind and pressing backward achieves this automatically as the horse spins. Much easier than trying to track the tail direction manually.

Fill the yellow gauge before your stamina runs out. If thrown off, run up and interact to start again — the progress doesn't reset. You need at least 4–6 Endurance skill points before attempting legendary horses.

Food needed
◈
Universal strategy: Eat while attacking — food has no animation lock. Spam light attacks + heal simultaneously to tank through damage. Never rely on blocking; it drains stamina fast and some attacks deal damage through it. Learn to parry (L1/LB) instead.
Human / knight bosses Parry with L1/LB to interrupt and open a combo window. Heavy attacks (R2/RT) break their guard. Most can be grappled — drains stagger bar fast.
Monster bosses Dodge combos, attack in the recovery window after a miss. Target weak spots. Watch for Observation Learning prompts — hold observe to bank a free technique.
◈
Broken pillars in boss arenas deal 25–50% of a boss's HP when thrown. If a boss breaks one, use Nature's Grasp to pick it up and throw it. Most bosses are also vulnerable to Lariat attacks which stagger them easily.

General boss tips
Observation Learning

Watch bosses to earn free skills

Every boss has an Observation Learning prompt during unique animations. Hold the observe button to bank a technique. These skills cost zero Abyss Artifacts and persist through respecs. Don't skip optional bosses — they teach rare abilities unavailable elsewhere.
Super Armor & stagger

Know when to interrupt

Bosses have Super Armor on specific attacks — standard strikes won't interrupt these. You need Super Armor Break from Turning Slash Mastery or Stab Mastery to interrupt them. Watch for the Super Armor animation and back off rather than trading hits.
Difficulty was patched

Post-patch 1.00.03 changes

Pearl Abyss reduced HP and attack of several bosses. Stamina consumption for blocking reduced. Stun gauge now accumulates faster on successful parries. Boss weakness effects display without needing prior knowledge. Game is more forgiving than at launch.
Weapon tip for all bosses

Tauria + Nature's Echo = strongest

Tauria Curved Sword (Crowcaller drop, Ch. 5) + Nature's Echo skill (Green tree) is the highest DPS combo in the mid-game. Nature's Echo duplicates heavy attacks — combined with Tauria's speed it melts health bars. Priority skill investment.

Abyss system — skill trees & artifact priorities
⚠
Each character has their own separate tree — points invested in Kliff don't carry to Damiane or Oongka. Focus all Artifacts on Kliff first. Respec costs a Faded Abyss Artifact (rare) — plan carefully before spending.
Blue tree — Endurance

Armed combat, dodges, attacks, gliding

Goes up to Lv. 16. The heart of your DPS. Invest heavily early — Stamina 200 threshold unlocks faster climbing speed and extended dodge chains. Essential for all combat styles.
Red tree — Health

Max HP, mobility, aerial skills

Goes up to Lv. 18. Raw survival. Health Lv. 4 is a minimum before any mid-game boss. More HP = more room to heal while attacking. Aerial skills unlock here too.
Green tree — Mind

Energy, counters, stun, Force Palm

Combat control tree. Nature's Echo (duplicates heavy attacks), Nature's Snare (roots enemies), Force Palm upgrades live here. High value after core Blue/Red investments are done.

Recruit skills matter

Match the right Greymane to each mission

Each recruit has skills (Farmer, Builder, Jeweler, Craftsman, etc.). Matching the required skill = better rewards and success rates. Some missions won't start without the right skill. Check Manage Comrades before assigning.

Dispatch runs on in-game time

Missions take 17–18 in-game hours. Sleep at the camp tent bed to advance time while you plan your next moves. Assign max recruits per mission — extra bodies generate bonus rewards on completion.
Camp stuck? Check journal

9 out of 10 times it's a missed Sunrise quest

If progression feels blocked, open Journal → Faction Quests → Greymane Grounds of the Sunrise. Find any quest with a camp icon. Talk to Marius whenever new Greymanes arrive — his quests unlock new people and facilities.
