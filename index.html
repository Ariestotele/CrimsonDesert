How it works

Slot-based only

Every item occupies exactly one slot regardless of size. No weight, no encumbrance — only slot count matters.
Stack overflow

Cap is 50 per slot

Mats, food and ammo stack to 50. Carrying 51+ of anything bleeds into a second slot. Craft often to consolidate.
◈
The Group feature consolidates same-type items visually — it doesn't increase your slot count, but makes the inventory screen much cleaner to navigate.
⚠
Shared inventory across Kliff, Damiane and Oongka. You can park a secondary character near a vendor to sell without fast-travelling yourself.
Bag types
Small Bag
+1 slot

50 Copper — buy every one

Almost every vendor in Pywel sells one. Permanent upgrade. Never skip a merchant without checking. Some vendors share pools — buy immediately.
Medium Bag
+3 slots

Quests only — not sold

Best slot-per-effort in the game. Greymane Commissions (27 available → up to +81 slots), Faction Quests, Hernand requests. Prioritise these.
Large Bag
+5 – +9 slots

Story milestones only

Tied to unlocking characters. Damiane (Ch. 3) and Oongka (Ch. 7) each grant large bags automatically.

Trading system overview
⚠
Traders only buy in bulk — you need at least 25 of a specific item per sale at Trading Posts. Single items go to Black Markets at much lower rates.
Step 01
Collect Trade Goods
Blue items found in chests, tables, cabinets while exploring. High-value: Calligraphic Paintings, Tobacco, Ceramics, Red Ginseng.
Step 02
Glenbright Manor
Complete the investigation of Glenbright Manor story quest first — this unlocks the ability to package goods at camp.
Step 03
Package via Carl
Carl (Provisions Keeper) at Greymane Camp → Manage Provisions → Package. Costs 100 Copper per item.
Step 04
Load Wagon via Brice
Talk to Brice to get your wagon. Load packaged goods from inventory or camp storage. Build Brice's workshop to upgrade wagon capacity.
Step 05
Drive to Trading Post
Check map for "High" price percentage before committing to a route. Goldleaf Guildhouse unlocks after Kailok the Hornsplitter boss.
✓
Never return with an empty wagon. Buy local goods from the destination post and route to another — that's where trading income compounds. Check the map's "High" price filter before you drive.

Early game priority checklist
⚠
Click items to mark them done. Checklist is saved in your browser session.
⚑ Do immediately (Hernand / Ch. 1)
✓
Ring the Hernand Bell Tower ASAP
Reveals a massive chunk of the map and all nearby points of interest. Every city has one — ring them whenever you enter a new region.
✓
Buy every Small Bag from Hernand vendors ASAP
50 Copper each. Multiple vendors in Hernand each carry one. Check every merchant — some share pools, so buy immediately when you see it.
✓
Cook Hearty Grilled Meat at the butcher bonfire ASAP
Buy meat from the Hernand butcher → use the Bonfire/Cooking Pot right beside him. 200+ HP regen. Best early food, very cheap. Stack 50 before any boss.
✓
Buy Pickaxe + Logging Axe from vendors ASAP
Required to gather ore and timber for gear refinement. Buy these before you leave Hernand for the first time.
✓
Get Palmar Pill recipe — Shadow's Whisper Cave ASAP
North of Hernand. Recipe on a table inside. Palmar Pills auto-revive you at 30% HP during boss fights — your single best tool for surviving hard encounters.
✓
Invest Abyss Artifacts into Health and Stamina first ASAP
Don't dump into attack. Stamina 200 threshold unlocks faster climbing + extended dodges. Extra Health is the make-or-break for most early bosses. Attack can come later.
⚑ Early priorities (Ch. 1–2)
✓
Complete all Hernand Commission quests Ch. 1
Your best early source of Medium Bags (+3 slots each). Renee's, Rhett's, Turnali's requests — do all of them before leaving Hernand. Check notice boards too.
✓
Learn and use Blinding Flash skill Ch. 1
Early unlock. Reveals Abyss Nexuses, solves puzzles, blinds enemies in combat, and sets environmental objects (vines) on fire. Also detects Sealed Abyss Artifacts with a blue glint.
✓
Collect all Sealed Abyss Artifacts you find Ch. 1–2
Challenge progress only counts AFTER pickup — not retroactively. Grab every one you see (blue glint along roads). Check Journal → Challenges to track them. Required for trophies.
✓
Use Grindstone + Anvil (Trial After Trial camp) Ch. 2
Sharpen weapons at Grindstone, repair armor at Anvil — maxes your gear's Reinforcement stat (visible when hovering equipped gear). Noticeable Attack and Defense gain at zero cost.
✓
Do Estate in Dismay (House Roberts) — unlocks early Ch. 2 Priority Side
Rewards: Gold Vein Map, Mining Knuckledrill, Azurite ×3, Bloodstones ×3, Engraved Gold Earring. One of the best early side quest rewards in the game.
✓
Unlock fast travel Abyss Nexus points aggressively Ongoing
Some chapter transitions require long travel between regions. Tag every Nexus point you walk past — saves huge amounts of backtracking later. Some require puzzles or specific steps to unlock.
✓
Rob Hernand Bank + steal Lioncrest Manor gold bar Ch. 1–2
Bank: need mask + key → 40+ Silver. Lioncrest Manor gold bar: steal it and exchange at the bank for 500 Silver. These two are exceptions to the "avoid crime" rule — the loot is worth it.
⚑ Mid-game priorities (Ch. 3+)
✓
Establish Greymane Camp at Howling Hill immediately Ch. 3
Unlocks Private Storage (240 slots), Greymane Commissions (27 bags = up to +81 slots), Brice's wagon workshop, and Damiane. Most important chapter milestone for inventory.
✓
Recruit all Greymane survivors → do all 27 Commissions Ch. 3
Each survivor recruited via Marius adds more Commission requests. 27 total = up to +81 slots from Medium Bags alone. This is your main mid-game slot farming route.
✓
Do NOT invest Abyss Artifacts into Damiane or Oongka yet Ch. 3–7
Focus all artifacts on Kliff until your build feels complete. Secondary characters' skill trees can wait until you have artifacts to spare.
✓
Park Damiane near Hernand — use as a remote vendor Ch. 3+
Inventory is shared across all three characters. Switch to Damiane and sell without fast-travelling Kliff. Saves significant travel time when looting-heavy exploring.
✓
Get Sustained Supply Arrow from Lumiel at Pororin Village Ch. 4
Unlimited arrows — they don't run out. Accessible after The Trembling Woods faction quest unlocks Pororin Forest Village.
Key mechanics the game doesn't explain well
01
Waterfall traversal

Waterfalls will knock you back if you walk through them. Use Stab (RB+Y / R1+△ / LSHIFT+RMB) right in front of the waterfall — Kliff parts the water with his blade and passes through. There's almost always treasure behind them.

02
Stacked rock towers = hidden treasure

If you see a small tower of stacked rocks in front of a waterfall or cliff face, that marks a passage or hidden chest. Either unique equipment or rare ore — always worth checking.

03
Blinding Flash has hidden uses

Beyond combat, Blinding Flash can set environmental objects (vines, barriers) on fire. Just aim the light at the obstacle. Also cooks meat if you drop it on the ground — no campfire needed.

04
Focus (slow-time) — don't forget it exists

Kliff's slow-time ability is introduced during a busy cutscene and never mentioned again. Bind it to an accessible button and use it liberally — it creates windows to land heavy combos or dodge otherwise unavoidable hits.

05
Sky Islands for fast travel shortcuts

On the map press R3 (PC: Mouse Wheel) to view the sky map. You can skydive from the nearest sky island fast travel point to any nearby ground location quickly — faster than riding for frequently visited spots without ground Nexuses.

06
Height climbing without Stamina drain

Use double jump + three Aerial Force Palms to reach considerable height without spending any Stamina. If stamina runs out mid-climb, immediately eat any stamina-restoring food — it refills instantly and you can continue.

07
Boss strategy that always works

Bosses are designed so you WILL take damage. Heal while attacking with light attacks. For blockable (human) bosses: parry with L1/LB, then combo. For shield users: R2/RT heavy attacks break guard. For monsters: dodge combos, punish windows. Bring at least 50 cooked food and hold Palmar Pills for the final phase.

08
Hold attack, don't tap

Hold R1/RB for continuous attacks instead of tapping each time. Much more efficient combat rhythm — tapping is the default instinct but holding keeps your combo momentum going.

09
Best early skills to unlock

Nature's Echo, Quick Swap, and Blinding Flash are the highest-priority early unlocks. Best early weapons: Tauria's Curved Sword (Crowcaller boss) and Sword of the Lord (Kailok boss). Play on controller if on PC — KB+M is notably harder.

10
Check Notifications for missed knowledge

Open Notifications from the main menu — it lists everything that's appeared and can redirect you to the specific menu section if you missed a knowledge entry or objective. Use this to stay organised as the quest list balloons.
